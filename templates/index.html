Problem Statement

Despite Rwanda's agricultural potential, smallholder farmers face significant challenges arising from outdated farming practices, inadequate trainings, and limited access to technology.

Approximately 70% of Rwanda's population engages in agriculture (World Bank, 2022), mostly in rural areas where the farming landscape is characterized by low productivity and poor income, particularly during key planting and harvest seasons (FAO, 2021).

These issues stem from a lack of access to affordable modern farming techniques and insufficient training programs (Rwanda Ministry of Agriculture, 2020), resulting in the inability of farmers to meet both local and international market demands.

Our 2037 Vision

"Addressing this issue is crucial for enhancing food security and fostering economic growth. Our aim is to reduce the productivity gap to possible low levels by 2037 by implementing targeted training and affordable tech-based farming solutions."

Our Solution

Our solution is a mobile app and physical toolkit that helps smallholder farmers identify and solve crop diseases, pest problems, and soil issues by simply taking a photo.

By receiving an instant AI-powered diagnosis and treatment advice in their local language, farmers can act quickly. For those without internet, our printed manuals and affordable soil pH detectors ensure no one is left behind.

The Team: As a group of six students—including three app designers and a practicing farm owner—we build tools that are practical, affordable, and field-tested.

Our Prototype: AI-Powered Farm Guide

Our core technology is an AI-based mobile application that empowers smallholder farmers with an intelligent assistant. By simply taking a photo, the app provides an instant diagnosis of crop diseases, pests, and soil health issues.

  • Instant AI Diagnosis via camera.
  • Advice in local languages.
  • Comprehensive, step-by-step cultivation guides for key crops.

Development Status: This prototype is currently in progress and actively developing in real farming environments.
